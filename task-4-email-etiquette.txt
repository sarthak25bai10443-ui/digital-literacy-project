Social Media Do's and Don'ts for College Students
Category
	Do's (✅)
	Don'ts (❌)
	Privacy
	Adjust your privacy settings to control who sees your posts.
	Don’t share your home address, phone number, or current location.
	Professionalism
	Curate a profile that you would be comfortable showing a future employer.
	Don’t post photos or comments involving illegal activities or substance abuse.
	Engagement
	Fact-check information before sharing it to prevent spreading misinformation.
	Don’t engage in "flame wars" or respond to trolls with aggression.
	Mental Health
	Take regular breaks from social media to focus on real-world connections.
	Don’t compare your "behind-the-scenes" life to everyone else's "highlight reel."
	Etiquette
	Ask for permission before posting photos or videos of other people.
	Don’t vent about professors, classmates, or your workplace online.

The Scenario: A Misinterpreted Email Loop
In a professional setting, a team member once sent a brief, one-sentence email to a colleague: "We need to talk about the project updates immediately." Because the sender omitted a greeting and used the word "immediately," the recipient interpreted the tone as angry and disciplinary. This led to the recipient feeling anxious and defensive during the subsequent meeting, which stifled creative collaboration and wasted time addressing "perceived" tension.
What could have been done differently?
The sender should have applied basic email etiquette by including a polite greeting and providing context to soften the tone—for example, "Hi [Name], I’m excited about the progress! Could we chat briefly to sync on the final steps?" Adding a few words of context prevents the recipient from "filling in the blanks" with negative assumptions, ensuring the communication remains productive rather than stressful.
